Future of genomic epidemiology

The COVID-19 pandemic has pushed the field perhaps ~5 years into the future

  • 2013-16 Ebola in West Africa: 29k confirmed cases and 1610 genomes
  • 2015-17 Zika in the Americas: 223k confirmed cases and 942 genomes
  • 2018-19 seasonal flu in US: 290k confirmed cases and 8864 genomes
  • 2020-21 COVID-19 pandemic: 162M confirmed cases and 1.6M genomes

Post-pandemic future

  • Ability to analyze genomic data is key to a world in which SARS-CoV-2 continues to circulate
  • Of primary importance: (1) picking vaccine strains and (2) tracking and responding to outbreaks
  • Due to the pandemic, there has been a huge growth in sequencing capacity in labs around the world, which will result in a large shift towards routine sequencing of other pathogens

My proposed research program

  • Two major threads: "Evolutionary forecasting" and "Genomic outbreak investigation"
  • Continue to instantiate research innovations into the Nextstrain software platform for broader use by the community
