Epidemic process

Sample some infections

Sequence viral genomes and determine phylogeny

Sequence viral genomes and determine phylogeny

This approach allows us to learn about outbreaks and epidemics

Ebola
Zika

On Jan 19, 2020, the first 13 viral genomes from Wuhan and Bangkok showed little genetic diversity

Data from CAMS, China CDC, Fudan University, Hubei CDC, Thai MOPH, WIV; Figure from nextstrain.org

Allowed us to infer introduction between Nov 15 and Dec 15 and subsequent rapid human-to-human spread

Bedford et al. 2020. Technical Report.

To date, over 17M SARS-CoV-2 genomes have been shared and we've tracked evolution in real-time at nextstrain.org

Wealth of genome data represents a foundational resource for tracking viral evolution and epidemiology

Though we're still only scratching the surface of this data, in large part due to methodological limitations

Sequence language models have amazing potential

Evo2 is trained on 9.3 trillion DNA bases and predicts mutation effects, learns biological features and can generate quasi-life-like sequences

Brixi et al. 2025. bioRxiv.

Directly model the evolutionary process as movement through high-dimensional latent space

With model in hand, we can better tackle

  • Evolutionary forecasting
    Which new COVID variant will emerge? Will H5N1 mutate to become human transmissible?
  • Patterns of spread
    Given early outbreak sequences, where are infections today?
  • Pan-pathogen surveillance via metagenomics
    Which anomolies represent threats?

I generally hope to be moving surveillance towards something closer to weather forecasting with always-on models projecting current fronts (epidemic waves) and incipient storms (outbreaks)
